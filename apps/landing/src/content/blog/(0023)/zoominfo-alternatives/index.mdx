[ZoomInfo](https://zoominfo.com) is still the reference database for US B2B data. It is also an annual contract that many teams struggle to justify, coverage outside the US is uneven, and by the time you export a list the data has often aged. Those three complaints drive most of the searches for an alternative.

One thing to settle first: no single vendor wins on coverage everywhere. Every database is strong in some segment and thin elsewhere. That is why the most reliable setup we see is a waterfall: try provider A, fall through to B and C, and pay only when someone returns the data. Keep that in mind while comparing the single-vendor options below.

Disclosure: we build [pipe0](https://pipe0.com), listed first.

## pipe0

pipe0 is the waterfall model from the intro, productized. Try several providers per lookup, pay only the one that returned a result, and run it from whichever surface fits: a [spreadsheet UI](/docs/sheets) that holds up to 2 million rows, a [REST API](/docs) for your own product or scripts, and an MCP server so [AI agents like Claude Code and ChatGPT](/docs/sheets/ai-agents) can run enrichment themselves. Failed lookups cost nothing. Plans start at $49/mo with 20 free credits to test coverage on your own list, which is the only benchmark that matters.

## Apollo.io

The most common ZoomInfo replacement. Apollo pairs a large contact database with sequencing and a dialer at per-seat prices that undercut ZoomInfo by a wide margin. The trade: data quality is more variable, and you are buying an all-in-one platform whether or not you need the outreach half.

## Cognism

Cognism's pitch is Europe: GDPR-conscious sourcing and phone-verified mobile numbers, where ZoomInfo's coverage is weakest. It sells on annual contracts like ZoomInfo, so it is a vendor swap rather than a pricing-model escape. Shortlist it if EMEA pipeline is where your data pain lives.

## Lusha

Lusha is the lightweight option: a browser extension and a simple credit model for looking up contacts one prospect or one small list at a time. Small teams get going in an afternoon. It is not built for bulk enrichment or workflow automation.

## UpLead

UpLead sells verified emails on pay-as-you-go credits with a real-time verification step at export. No contract, decent accuracy, smaller database. A pragmatic pick when your volume is modest and unpredictable.

## LeadIQ

LeadIQ focuses on the moment of capture: an SDR finds a prospect on LinkedIn and LeadIQ writes the enriched contact into the CRM and the sequence. It replaces the workflow around ZoomInfo more than the database itself.

## Clay

Clay is not a database. It is an enrichment table that queries many databases (including some of the above) in waterfalls, the same multi-provider model pipe0 uses. Coverage becomes a function of how many providers you stack rather than one vendor's dataset. Where Clay falls short and who competes with it is its own topic: see our [Clay alternatives](/blog/clay-alternatives) guide and the [pipe0 vs Clay](/compare/pipe0-vs-clay) comparison.

## How to choose

Run the same 200 rows through your two or three finalists and count found emails, valid phones, and cost per found record. Coverage claims in marketing pages do not survive contact with a real list. If your list is international or your titles are niche, weight the waterfall options; single databases fall off fastest exactly there.
